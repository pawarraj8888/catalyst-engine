Methodology & honest findings

What this is

Catalyst Engine tracks ~250 US large-caps across Healthcare, Consumer, Tech, and Industrials. Each weekday morning a scheduled job scores upcoming earnings catalysts and appends new calls to a public log. Each weekday evening, calls whose event date has passed are resolved against realized 1-day price moves and classified HIT or MISS against the trailing 8-quarter baseline. Misses receive a written post-mortem.

What the backtest says

On free large-cap data with rule-based scoring, the V0 engine performs at approximately the efficient-markets baseline. Bucket 4-6 trends positive but N is too small to call a result. The infrastructure, PIT discipline, and 10b5-1 filtering are the contribution; the live track record is the deliverable.

Findings worth keeping

Large-cap insider buys are rare: 1,760 open-market buys versus 103,345 sales across 5 years. The Cohen-Malloy-Pomorski cluster-buy signal fires only 3 times across the entire universe. The literature edge lives in small-caps, not the S&P 500. Vol clustering signals are arbed out. Net selling above $5M (excluding 10b5-1 pre-planned trades) shows weak directional signal but below significance.
