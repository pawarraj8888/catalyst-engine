When a company reports earnings, its stock can jump or drop. Most prints are quiet. A few cause big moves. MoveScanner watches 241 large US companies and tries to guess, in advance, which ones are about to have a big move — in either direction.

Every weekday, the system scores each upcoming earnings report from 0 (nothing unusual) to 10 (looks like a big move is coming). After each company reports, we check what actually happened and mark the call a hit or a miss. The full log is public — every prediction is time-stamped before the event, every outcome is recorded after.

What MoveScanner does and doesn't do

What it does: Predicts whether a stock's reaction to its earnings report will be bigger or smaller than that stock's usual reaction. Think of it as a calendar prioritizer — "of the 30 companies reporting next week, here are the 5 worth paying attention to."

What it doesn't do: Predict which direction the stock will move. A big move can go up or down. MoveScanner only flags magnitude, not direction. Direction depends on whether the earnings beat or miss expectations, and that's a separate question that would need fundamentals data, options data, or sell-side estimates to answer.

Why this is honest: On free data and large US stocks, predicting direction reliably is genuinely hard — the market is efficient at pricing most of what's public. Predicting magnitude is easier because volatility patterns persist. MoveScanner is built around that asymmetry on purpose.
